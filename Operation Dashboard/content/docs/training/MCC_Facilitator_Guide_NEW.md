# **MCC VR CCTV Training System**

## **Facilitator Guide**

**Instructor Script for On-Site Training Session**

Client: MCC / UAE Supreme Council for National Security

Date: April 2026

Classification: Confidential

# **Pre-Session Preparation Checklist**

Verify device and software readiness. The training room is permanently set up in a grid layout and ready to use.

## **Device and Software Verification**

* Verify with Training Administrator: all operator PCs are powered on, responsive, and running the MCC application
* Confirm all VR headsets are charged to full capacity and optically clean
* Check EmotiBit sensors: all charged with solid LED indicators (no flashing)
* Verify supervisor PC is configured, logged in, and ready to host sessions

## **Printed Materials**

* Quick Start Cards (1 per operator, 1 per supervisor)
* Troubleshooting Flowcharts (1 per station)
* Assessment Checklists (1 per participant)

## **Spare Equipment**

* Extra EmotiBit sensor charged and tested
* Extra face cushions (for hygiene and comfort)
* Cleaning wipes for headsets

## **Support Availability**

* Confirm Training Administrator is available on-site for technical support during the entire session

# **Block 1: Get Oriented**

**30 minutes (0:00-0:30)**

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Time** | **Duration** | **Activity** |
| 0:00-0:10 | 10 min | Welcome and System Overview |
| 0:10-0:30 | 20 min | Hardware Familiarization |

## **0:00-0:10: Welcome and System Overview**

### **Talking Points**

* Introduce the MCC VR Training System: what it is, who built it (for UAE Supreme Council for National Security), and what it does
* Point to the stations arranged around the room. Each operator will work at one station with their own PC and VR headset
* Demonstrate the EmotiBit sensor physically: show how it straps on the wrist, explain the LED indicator means it is transmitting
* Explain that operators will put on VR headsets during hardware setup and keep them on for the entire session
* Address biometrics directly: heart rate, skin conductance, and temperature are recorded for training analysis only, not surveillance

### **Watch For**

* Confusion or anxiety about wearing the headset
* Questions about what data is collected
* Reluctance from anyone unfamiliar with VR

### **What to Do**

* Be transparent about biometrics from the start. This builds trust
* Normalize questions. Let people ask freely
* If nervousness persists, offer a brief hands-on demo before the full session

## **0:10-0:30: Hardware Familiarization**

### **Talking Points**

* Guide each operator through strapping on the EmotiBit: show the wrist strap, confirm the LED turns solid green
* Walk operators through putting on the VR headset: adjust the head strap, use the dial to fit snugly but comfortably
* Once headsets are on, operators will keep them on from this point forward
* Supervisor: confirm your PC connection to the session, verify you can see the lobby on your dashboard

### **Watch For**

* Operators uncomfortable with VR or reporting dizziness
* EmotiBit LED issues or connection failures
* Operators struggling to find a comfortable headset fit

### **What to Do**

* Walk the room actively. Do not sit back. Be present and helpful
* Help anyone struggling with straps or fit. Normalize asking for help
* If hardware issues arise, call the Training Administrator immediately. Do not troubleshoot yourself

# **Block 2: Operators Learn by Doing**

**75 minutes (0:30-1:45)**

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Time** | **Duration** | **Activity** |
| 0:30-0:45 | 15 min | Join Session and Lobby |
| 0:45-1:15 | 30 min | First Live Scenario (Airport) |
| 1:15-1:30 | 15 min | Debrief the Airport Run |
| 1:30-1:45 | 15 min | Break |

## **0:30-0:45: Join Session and Lobby**

### **Talking Points**

* From inside their VR headsets, guide operators to launch the MCC application
* Have them enter their name when prompted in the lobby
* Operators will see the lobby environment and other participants
* Supervisor: create a new session and select the Airport scenario
* Walk operators toward readiness: watch for all operator names appearing on the supervisor's lobby screen

### **Watch For**

* Operators who cannot find the application
* Lobby connection issues or names not appearing on supervisor screen
* Headset fit or comfort problems now that they are on

### **What to Do**

* Confirm all operators show up on the supervisor's lobby screen before proceeding
* If connection issues occur, ask the Training Administrator for support immediately

## **0:45-1:15: First Live Scenario (Airport)**

### **Talking Points**

* The Airport scenario focuses on detecting suspicious item placement and subject behaviors related to IED deployment
* Narrate key moments: Watch for the male placing the backpack near Camera 01. Notice the woman filming on her phone at Camera 03
* Guide operators on marking: press the marking button when they see something suspicious, place a waypoint on the suspect, add notes if time allows
* Expect alarms: multiple alarms may trigger during the scenario. This is normal. Operators should acknowledge and log responses
* This is their first run. Mistakes are expected and valuable

### **Watch For**

* Tunnel vision: operators staring at one camera for too long and missing activity elsewhere
* Slow alarm response: operators not reacting quickly to audio/visual alerts
* Confusion about how to use marking tools
* Headset fogging or comfort issues

### **What to Do**

* Let operators make mistakes. Do not interrupt to correct them. Observation and learning from error is the goal
* Note your observations for the debrief
* If an operator is significantly disoriented or uncomfortable, remove their headset briefly, assess, and return if they are ready

## **1:15-1:30: Debrief the Airport Run**

### **Talking Points**

* Pull up one operator's auto-generated report on the supervisor's screen and share with the group
* Walk through the report sections: composite score (0-100), the 4 dimensions (Detection Accuracy 40%, False Positives 20%, Timeliness 20%, Attention & Gaze 20%)
* Review the radar chart showing strengths and gaps across the four dimensions
* Show the heatmap: areas of the scenario where this operator spent attention
* Read the AI recommendations: the system flags specific areas for improvement
* Highlight one strength per operator, then one area to work on
* Normalize low first-run scores. Everyone is learning

### **Watch For**

* Defensiveness about low scores
* Operators comparing scores or becoming discouraged

### **What to Do**

* Frame scores as feedback, not judgment
* Acknowledge effort and improvement potential
* Keep the tone constructive and encouraging

## **1:30-1:45: Break**

### **Process**

* Operators remove VR headsets briefly
* Swap face cushions if needed for hygiene and comfort
* Offer water and a moment to rest
* Headsets go back on before Block 3 begins (around 1:45)

# **Block 3: Deeper Practice**

**60 minutes (1:45-2:45)**

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Time** | **Duration** | **Activity** |
| 1:45-2:15 | 30 min | Second Scenario (Embassy or Mall) |
| 2:15-2:30 | 15 min | Supervisor Focus: Replay and Reporting |
| 2:30-2:45 | 15 min | Operators Self-Review |

## **1:45-2:15: Second Scenario (Embassy or Mall)**

### **Talking Points**

* Less hand-holding this time. Operators run more independently
* Choose Embassy or Mall based on group readiness. Both are valuable training contexts
* Supervisor: practice reading biometrics in real time. Watch heart rate and skin conductance spikes when operators detect suspicious activity
* Supervisor: observe eye tracking overlays on the dashboard. See where operators are looking and how attention shifts
* Supervisor: trigger one distraction mid-scenario to test operator recovery (a commotion in the background, an alert)

### **Watch For**

* Improvement from Block 2: better alarm response, more confident marking, fewer tunnel vision moments
* Operators using marking tools naturally, not struggling
* Supervisor engaging with dashboard panels and biometrics display

### **What to Do**

* Step back. Let operators and supervisor work more autonomously
* Observe and be ready to intervene only if safety or technical issues arise

## **2:15-2:30: Supervisor Focus: Replay and Reporting**

### **Talking Points**

* Operators remove headsets briefly and observe (they stay in the room, watching the supervisor screen)
* Supervisor: open the post-session dashboard and select an operator
* Walk through the tabs: Eye Data, Biometrics, Marking, Activity Logs, Alarms
* Show the replay system: scrub through the timeline, adjust playback speed, toggle overlays on and off
* Generate a PDF report: show how to customize sections and export

### **Watch For**

* Supervisor struggling with replay controls
* Confusion about what each tab represents
* Operators asking questions about their own data

### **What to Do**

* Demo the replay system slowly and deliberately
* Explain the purpose of each tab
* Show how supervisors will use this tool in their day-to-day work

## **2:30-2:45: Operators Self-Review**

### **Process**

* Hand each operator their printed report
* Give them 10 minutes to read, identify their top strengths, and note AI-flagged improvement areas
* Do not require sharing; this is personal reflection

### **Watch For**

* Questions about report content or scoring logic
* Operators who do not understand their improvement areas

### **What to Do**

* Answer report questions directly and clearly
* Reinforce that improvement is a multi-session process

# **Block 4: Prove It and Wrap Up**

**45 minutes (2:45-3:30)**

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Time** | **Duration** | **Activity** |
| 2:45-3:15 | 30 min | Practical Assessment |
| 3:15-3:30 | 15 min | Review and Handover |

## **2:45-3:15: Practical Assessment**

### **Talking Points**

* Each operator runs a short scenario segment (15-20 minutes). This is a low-pressure assessment, not a final exam
* Supervisor hosts the session independently. You observe and score using Assessment Checklists
* Do not provide hints or help unless someone is completely lost
* Focus on: alarm response time, marking accuracy, attention distribution, confidence

### **Watch For**

* Who works independently without prompting
* Who needs guidance or reassurance
* Alarm response quality and speed
* Operators applying what they learned in Block 2 and 3

### **What to Do**

* Score honestly using the Assessment Checklist
* Note names and scores for the final handover
* If someone struggles, flag them for follow-up mentoring in the onboarding plan

## **3:15-3:30: Review and Handover**

### **Process**

* All headsets off. Everyone seated and comfortable
* Briefly share assessment results: highlight standout performers and anyone needing follow-up
* Do not shame or embarrass anyone
* Hand out all printed materials: Software Guide sections, Quick Reference Cards, Troubleshooting Flowcharts, Assessment Checklists
* Explain that the onboarding SOP covers ongoing development: regular scenarios, supervisor mentoring, competency reviews
* Answer final questions
* Thank participants for their effort and engagement

# **Handling Common Questions**

### **Why is my score low?**

First sessions always produce lower scores. The system tracks improvement over time. Focus on the specific areas flagged by the AI in your report. That is where your effort will have the most impact.

### **Can we change the scenarios?**

Scenarios are pre-configured for this training phase. New scenarios can be added in future phases based on operational needs.

### **What if the headset is uncomfortable?**

Adjust the head strap for a snugger or looser fit. Use the comfort dial on the side. If the forehead area is sore, swap face cushions. Let me know if it is still problematic.

### **Who sees my biometric data?**

Only the supervisor during the session and in the post-session report. Biometric data is used for training analysis and personal feedback. It is not shared beyond this group.

### **What if something technical breaks?**

That is what the Training Administrator is here for. Raise your hand or tell me immediately. Do not try to fix it yourself.

### **How long do we wear the headset?**

About three hours total, with a 15-minute break in the middle. You will have time to rest your eyes and adjust if needed.

# **Post-Session Actions**

* Collect all Assessment Checklists
* Note any technical issues encountered and brief the Training Administrator before you leave
* Confirm next steps with participants: when regular training sessions will begin, how to access the onboarding SOP, who their mentor will be
* Thank participants and reinforce the positive. End on a confident note.

# **Quick Instructor Reference**

## **Key Principles**

* 30% talking, 70% hands-on learning
* Mistakes are learning. Do not interrupt to correct
* Be present and visible. Walk the room constantly
* Normalize questions. Make it safe to ask for help
* Be transparent about data. Address biometrics early
* Flag blockers immediately to Training Administrator
* End strong. Acknowledge effort and build confidence

## **Emergency Contacts**

* Technical issue: Training Administrator (on-site)
* Operator medical or safety concern: stop session, notify Training Administrator, follow site protocols
* Hardware failure: do not troubleshoot. Call Training Administrator
* Session connection lost: wait 30 seconds, try reconnect. If not restored, call Training Administrator
