# 1. VR Training Solution Demo Zone

The VR Training section of the MCC booth contains the full live demonstration setup: an operator station where a visitor (or a dedicated MCC demo operator) runs through the Airport scenario in VR, and a supervisor station showing the dashboard with live metrics, biometric data, and eye-tracking visualisations. The setup is available in two configurations: a minimal setup (Option 1) and an expanded setup (Option 2).

## 1.1 Option 1: Minimal Setup

The minimal setup provides the core demonstration capability with two stations and their associated monitors. This is the required baseline for any booth configuration.

**Operator Station**

* 1x desk (120 cm wide, 70 cm deep minimum). Clean, cable-managed.
* 1x high-performance PC (under-desk or rear-mounted). Connected to the HTC Vive Focus Vision via wired link cable.
* 1x 27” or 32” monitor on the desk for the operator’s view during scenarios.
* 1x HTC Vive Focus Vision headset with link cable. Stored on a headset stand when not in use.
* 1x EmotiBit device (wrist-worn by the operator during demos). Connected to the biometric recording system.
* 1x keyboard and 1x mouse. The mouse is actively used during scenarios and needs a proper mouse pad and clear desk space.
* 1x comfortable chair (adjustable height).
* Cable management: all cables routed through desk grommets or under-desk cable trays.

**Supervisor Station**

* 1x desk (100-120 cm wide, 70 cm deep). Positioned beside or behind the operator station.
* 1x PC (under-desk). Running the server/supervisor software.
* 1x 27” or 32” monitor showing the supervisor dashboard: live feeds, operator progress, camera views, biometric data, and eye-tracking data.
* 1x keyboard and 1x mouse.
* 1x chair.

## 1.2 Option 2: Expanded Setup

The expanded setup adds attraction displays, a dedicated biometric waveform monitor, and a lead capture station to the minimal setup. This configuration enhances visitor engagement and data capture. Additional software development, effort, and budget are required for this option.

**Additional Items (on top of Option 1)**

* 2x 55” TV/Display for the attraction zone, wall-mounted or on freestanding stands, facing the aisle to attract passing visitors.
* 1x 24” or 27” monitor or large tablet dedicated to running the EmotiBit Oscilloscope software, showing the live waveform of the operator’s biometric data.
* 1x tablet (iPad or Android) for digital lead capture in the engagement zone.
* 1x high-top engagement table/counter at standing height for visitor interactions.
* 1x HDMI splitter or capture card to mirror VR output to an attraction display.

## 3.3 Emergency Kit (Booth Survival Box)

Keep a box under one of the desks containing the following. Pack it the night before.

* 2x spare HDMI cables (3 m), 1x spare USB-C cable, 1x spare power cable
* Gaffer tape (black), electrical tape, double-sided tape
* Velcro cable ties (pack of 20)
* Multi-tool or screwdriver set
* Portable phone charger / power bank
* Paracetamol, throat lozenges, hand sanitiser
* Snack bars and water bottles for staff
* Notebook and pens (physical backup for lead capture)
* Printed troubleshooting cheat sheet for the VR software
* Spare batteries (AA/AAA) if any peripherals need them
