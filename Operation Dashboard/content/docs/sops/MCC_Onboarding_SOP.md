# **1. Purpose**

This SOP establishes a repeatable procedure for onboarding new CCTV operators and supervisors to the MCC VR Training System. It ensures every new hire reaches operational competency through structured, mentor-led training before they work independently.

# **2. Scope**

Applies to all new operators and supervisors joining the MCC VR Training team. Does not cover IT infrastructure setup or system administration.

# **3. Definitions**

* Operator: Person who wears the VR headset and monitors simulated CCTV feeds during training scenarios
* Supervisor: Person who hosts training sessions, monitors operators in real time, and generates performance reports (no VR headset)
* Mentor: An experienced operator or supervisor assigned to guide a new hire through onboarding
* Composite Score: Automated performance score (0-100) generated after each training scenario
* Sign-Off: Formal confirmation that a new hire has met all competency requirements

# **4. Prerequisites**

* New hire has been assigned a workstation with all hardware and software pre-installed by IT
* A mentor in the same role has been identified and has capacity for 8 working days of guided support
* Printed materials available: User Manual (role-specific sections), Quick Reference Card, Troubleshooting Flowchart

# **5. Roles and Responsibilities**

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Role** | **Responsibilities** |
| Training Manager | Assigns mentor, tracks progress through all phases, approves sign-off, maintains training records |
| Mentor | Guides new hire through all 4 phases, completes competency checklist, recommends sign-off, documents progress |
| New Hire | Completes all required activities, achieves competency benchmarks, actively participates in all phases |
| IT Staff | Ensures workstation is configured and operational before Day 1, troubleshoots technical issues as needed |

# **6. Procedure**

## **6.1 Phase 1: Orientation (Day 1)**

* Welcome briefing: explain what the MCC VR Training System is, why it exists, and what the new hire's role will be
* Facility tour: show training room layout, workstation assignment, supply locations, and emergency exits
* Mentor introduction and assignment: ensure both mentor and new hire understand the commitment and complete the Mentor Assignment Form
* Printed materials handed over: provide User Manual (role-specific sections), Quick Reference Card, and Troubleshooting Flowchart
* System access verification: confirm workstation is set up correctly, software launches successfully, and EmotiBit sensor is available and functional

## **6.2 Phase 2: Guided Practice (Days 2 through 4)**

New hires follow one of two tracks based on their assigned role.

### **Operator Track**

* Day 2: Hardware setup under mentor observation. First VR session running the Airport scenario. Mentor talks through what to look for and how to respond to events.
* Day 3: Run Embassy and Mall scenarios. Practice marking entities and responding to alarms. Mentor provides feedback after each run.
* Day 4: Run Concert and Protest scenarios. Self-review performance reports with mentor present. Sit down with mentor to discuss improvement areas and strengths.

### **Supervisor Track**

* Day 2: Dashboard walkthrough with mentor. Observe an experienced supervisor hosting a live session with operators.
* Day 3: Host a live session with 1 to 2 operators under mentor guidance. Practice reading biometrics and triggering distractions correctly.
* Day 4: Generate reports and interpret data. Practice using the replay system. Conduct a mock debrief with the mentor acting as an operator.

## **6.3 Phase 3: Solo Practice (Days 5 through 7)**

New hire executes all tasks independently. Mentor observes but only steps in if the new hire is truly stuck. After each session, mentor completes the Competency Observation Checklist, documenting strengths and areas needing additional work.

## **6.4 Phase 4: Sign-Off (Day 8)**

### **For Operators:**

* Run a complete scenario independently from start to finish
* Achieve a score of 70 percent or above on the auto-generated performance report
* Pass the mentor's competency checklist
* Training Manager reviews all documentation and approves sign-off

### **For Supervisors:**

* Host a complete multi-operator session
* Generate reports and deliver a comprehensive debrief
* Pass assessment by mentor and an existing supervisor using the competency checklist
* Training Manager reviews all documentation and approves sign-off

### **If Competency Not Demonstrated:**

* Mentor identifies and documents specific gaps
* Mentor and new hire conduct targeted practice on those specific areas
* Reassessment scheduled within 5 working days
* No certification granted until competency is fully demonstrated

# **7. Records and Documentation**

All onboarding materials must be retained for compliance and training history purposes.

* Mentor Assignment Form: completed and signed by both mentor and new hire, filed with Training Manager
* Competency Checklist: completed by mentor during Phase 3 and 4, filed per new hire
* Progress Tracker: maintained throughout all phases, documents daily activities and observations
* Sign-Off Record: final approval added to training log once competency is confirmed
